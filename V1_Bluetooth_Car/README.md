# ESP32-Based-Bluetooth-Controlled-Car
ESP32-based Bluetooth controlled robotic car that connects to a smartphone for wireless navigation. Built using ESP32, L298N motor driver, 4 gear motors, 4-wheel plastic chassis, and dual 18650 batteries for efficient and responsive movement control.<br>

🚗✨ Excited to share my latest IoT project – an **ESP32-Based Bluetooth Controlled Car**!

I designed and built a smart robotic car that can be controlled directly from a smartphone via Bluetooth. The ESP32 acts as the brain of the system, receiving commands from the mobile application and controlling the movement of the car in different directions.
<br>
🔹 **Key Features:**<br>
✅ Wireless control using Bluetooth connectivity<br>
✅ Forward, Backward, Left, Right, and Stop movements<br>
✅ Compact and low-cost robotic car design<br>
✅ Easy to upgrade with additional sensors for obstacle avoidance and autonomous navigation<br>
<br>
🛠️ **Components Used:**<br>
• ESP32 Development Board<br>
• L298N Motor Driver Module<br>
• 4 × Gear Motors<br>
• 4 × Wheels<br>
• Plastic Car Chassis<br>
• 2 × 18650 Rechargeable Batteries<br>
• Jumper Wires and Connecting Accessories<br>
<br>

🔸 Embedded Systems Programming<br>
